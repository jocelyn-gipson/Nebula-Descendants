In a land of richest man who ever lived, land locked by rolling desert sands, and covered with Baobab and Shea trees, comes the Dogon. An ancient poeples of Mali, exsisting for centuries, guided be their ancestorial spirits. Dogon culture is rooted in oral traditions, modern astrology, and art. Deep mysteries of gods and stars are transformed into art and dance, this is what the Dogon are known for.

Art

Traditional Dogon Door

The carved doors and doorways portray ancestral figures to protect the people of the village and their families. Due to isolation, Dogon art has remained one of the most authentic on the African continent and it has remained mostly intact

Dogon Handcarved Stool

From everyday, simple to the ornanate ceremonial seeting place of high priest and political leaders, Dogon stools are created. Supported by figures representing ancestors or nommo, those with a post in the middle are linked with Dogon's mythology. The seat represents heaven, the base represents the earth, and the post represents a tree, linking the two.

Mythology

Nommo

Dogon often refer to their ancestors as the Nommo. The Nommo were considered "Teachers" and "Monitor" which is a similar to the Sumerian and Assyrian Gods the Annunaki.

Astrology

The mythology of the the dogon aligns with other ancients cultures in the world. In fact, the word Dogon in Sumerian is in reference to great ruler or fish or fishing. The Dogon tribe has recorded astrological events in the form of art. The disk represents the an astrological event that took place in 3123BC.

Boys to Men

Researchers have photographed the Dogon Cave paintings for more than 100 years, but the Dogon Tribe have been paintings rocks for much longer. Each image painted by finger, represents a special part of a boy's intiation into manhood. This secretly held intiation is one of many restricted ceremonies performed by the tribe and is held in high regard by tribe elders. Accompaning the painting is the circumsison stone also used in the right of passage.
