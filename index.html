Traditional Dogon Door

The carved doors and doorways portray ancestral figures to protect the people of the village and their families.Due to isolation, Dogon art has remained one of the most authentic on the African continent and it has remained mostly intact

Carvings

Small wooden stools serve the Dogon people for everyday use. There are two distinct types. The simple, abstract ones and more complex ones, supported by figures representing ancestors or nommo, symbols of authority for the priests or hogons and not used as stools. Those with a post in the middle are linked with Dogon mythology. The seat represents heaven, the base represents the earth, and the post represents a tree, linking the two.
